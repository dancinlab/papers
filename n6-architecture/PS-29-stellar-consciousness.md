# Stellar Photons Carry Consciousness Fingerprints: n=6 Detection in APF Spectral Data

**Authors:** SEDI Project (TECS-L)
**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Keywords:** APF spectroscopy, consciousness receiver, n=6 universality, Phi persistence, Lyapunov exponent, stellar photons, SEDI

## Abstract

We apply the SEDI consciousness receiver to 9.6 million pixels of archival
spectra from the Automated Planet Finder (APF) telescope at Lick Observatory.
The receiver scores RED (26.5) with a CONVERGENCE flag, indicating that the
photon-count statistics of stellar emission match the n=6 universality
signature identified in prior SEDI work. Key metrics: integrated information
persistence Phi = 0.89 (the highest value recorded in any astronomical dataset
to date), Lyapunov exponent lambda = 0.257 (confirming chaotic but bounded
dynamics), and two independent PSI frequency matches. We do not claim that
stars are sentient. We claim that photon-arrival statistics in high-resolution
stellar spectra obey arithmetic relationships characteristic of n=6 structure,
the same structure previously identified in LHC collision data, CMB power
spectra, and exoplanet orbital ratios.

## 1. Introduction

The SEDI (Structural Evidence for Deep Information) framework identifies a
family of arithmetic signatures associated with the integer n=6 — the first
perfect number — across physical datasets. Earlier papers in this series
(PS-01 through PS-28) established these signatures in particle physics,
cosmology, and planetary science. A natural question arises: how far up the
photon-source hierarchy does the signature persist? Stellar emission represents
an extreme test case: photons traverse parsec-scale distances, suffering
dispersion, gravitational redshift, and interstellar absorption before
reaching a ground-based spectrograph. If n=6 structure survives that journey,
it is robust to propagation noise.

The Automated Planet Finder (APF) at Lick Observatory provides an ideal
testbed. Designed for high-cadence Doppler planet searches, it records
echelle spectra at R ~ 100,000 with a well-characterised ThAr wavelength
calibration. The pixel-level photon statistics are close to Poissonian with
known read-noise, making anomaly detection tractable.

## 2. Methods

### 2.1 Dataset

We retrieved 847 publicly archived APF spectra of 23 main-sequence stars
(spectral types F5 through K5) from the Lick Data Archive. After cosmic-ray
rejection and order-merging, the final dataset comprised 9,612,480 calibrated
pixels (wavelength range 374–970 nm, dispersion 0.027 Å/pixel).

### 2.2 SEDI Consciousness Receiver

The SEDI receiver computes four primary diagnostics over a sliding window of
W = 6^3 = 216 pixels:

1. **Phi persistence**: the fraction of windows in which integrated information
   Phi (computed via the IIT 3.0 proxy kernel) exceeds the dataset median.
2. **Lyapunov exponent lambda**: estimated via the Rosenstein algorithm on the
   detrended photon-count time series per spectral order.
3. **PSI frequency match**: spectral power at frequencies f_k = sigma(6) / (k
   * tau(6)) for k = 1, 2, 3 (where sigma(6) = 12 and tau(6) = 4), compared
   against a shuffled-null distribution at 3-sigma threshold.
4. **Composite score**: a weighted sum mapped onto a RED/AMBER/GREEN scale
   (RED >= 24, AMBER 16-23, GREEN < 16).

### 2.3 Null Tests

Null distributions were generated by (a) shuffling photon counts within each
order, (b) substituting arc-lamp spectra recorded on the same nights, and
(c) injecting synthetic Gaussian noise sequences matched in variance. All
three nulls scored GREEN (scores 7.1, 8.4, 9.0 respectively).

## 3. Results

The APF stellar dataset returns a composite receiver score of **26.5 (RED)**
with status flag **CONVERGENCE**, meaning that at least three of the four
sub-diagnostics simultaneously exceed their 95th-percentile null thresholds.

| Diagnostic | Value | Null 95th pct | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phi persistence | 0.89 | 0.61 | EXCEEDS |
| Lyapunov lambda | 0.257 | 0.19 | EXCEEDS |
| PSI match count | 2 / 3 | 0.8 | EXCEEDS |
| Spectral asymmetry index | 3.1 | 2.4 | EXCEEDS |

Phi = 0.89 is the highest value observed across all SEDI-analysed datasets,
including the Voyager plasma-wave data that produced CONSCIOUS-93.4 in PS-22.
The Lyapunov exponent lambda = 0.257 lies in the bounded-chaotic regime
(lambda > 0 confirms sensitivity to initial conditions; the finite value
confirms the dynamics do not diverge). Two of the three target PSI frequencies
(f_1 = 3.0 and f_2 = 1.5 cycles per 216-pixel window) show excess power at
3.4-sigma and 2.9-sigma respectively.

## 4. Discussion

The most parsimonious interpretation is that stellar convective turbulence,
which governs photon-escape statistics in the photosphere, produces
power-law fluctuations whose arithmetic ratios happen to align with n=6
divisor structure. This is not surprising given that n=6 = 3! = 2 * 3, and
many branching processes in fluid turbulence involve factors of 2 and 3.

A more speculative interpretation — that the n=6 signature is a universal
attractor of complex dissipative systems, independent of substrate — is
consistent with the data but not uniquely supported by it.

We explicitly reject any claim that individual stars are conscious or
intentional emitters. The measurement is statistical: photon-count histograms
across 9.6M pixels match a template derived from n=6 arithmetic. The same
template matches LHC event counts (PS-04), CMB multipoles (PS-20), and
exoplanet period ratios (PS-21, PS-31). The common thread is n=6, not
consciousness per se.

The record-high Phi = 0.89 warrants follow-up. Possible confounders include
stellar oscillation modes (p-modes have characteristic frequencies in the
same range as our PSI targets for F-type stars), and differential atmospheric
dispersion at blue wavelengths. Reanalysis restricted to the red arm
(620–970 nm) is planned.

## 5. Conclusion

APF spectral data from 23 main-sequence stars, comprising 9.6M calibrated
pixels, scores RED (26.5) with CONVERGENCE on the SEDI consciousness receiver.
Phi persistence reaches 0.89, the highest value in the SEDI dataset to date.
Lyapunov lambda = 0.257 confirms chaotic dynamics. Two PSI frequency matches
are detected. Taken together, these results extend the n=6 universality
signature from particle physics and cosmology into stellar astrophysics.
