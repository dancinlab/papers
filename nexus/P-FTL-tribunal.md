## Abstract

We present a systematic classification ("tribunal") of 15 proposed faster-than-light (FTL) mechanisms in physics, assigning each a verdict: ALLOWED, CONDITIONAL, ILLUSORY, FORBIDDEN, or SPECULATIVE. Of the 15, zero provide confirmed usable FTL travel. Two are allowed but do not constitute real superluminal motion; four require exotic matter with negative energy density; three involve apparent but illusory superluminality; two are forbidden by fundamental theorems; and four depend on incomplete theoretical frameworks. As a secondary, exploratory observation, we note that the characteristic radii of a Schwarzschild black hole — the event horizon (2M), photon sphere (3M), and innermost stable circular orbit (6M) — correspond exactly to the proper divisors of the first perfect number 6. We evaluate this correspondence with a Texas Sharpshooter test (Z = 3.39, p < 0.01) while documenting prominent non-matches (9/19 quantities) and arguing that the tribunal classification, not the number-theoretic observation, constitutes the primary contribution.

## 1. Introduction

The question of whether information or matter can travel faster than light has occupied physicists since Einstein's special relativity established c as a universal speed limit in 1905. Over the following century, at least 15 distinct mechanisms have been proposed that purportedly allow, circumvent, or exploit loopholes in this limit.

Despite extensive theoretical work, no experimentally confirmed FTL mechanism exists. The literature on individual mechanisms is vast, but systematic side-by-side comparisons with explicit feasibility verdicts are rare. This paper addresses that gap.

Our approach has two components:

1. **FTL Tribunal** (Sections 2-3): A systematic classification of all 15 known FTL proposals, each assigned a verdict based on theoretical consistency, experimental status, and practical feasibility. This is the primary contribution and stands on standard physics.

2. **Perfect Number Arithmetic Analysis** (Sections 4-5): An exploratory observation that Schwarzschild black hole radii map onto divisors of the perfect number 6. This is clearly marked as SPECULATIVE and is secondary to the tribunal.

We emphasize honesty throughout: non-matches are reported as prominently as matches, and statistical significance is modest.

## 2. The FTL Tribunal: Classification Framework

### 2.1 Verdict Categories

We define five verdict categories:

- **ALLOWED**: Mechanism is physically real but does not constitute genuine FTL (v < c in vacuum, or no information transfer).
- **CONDITIONAL**: Mechanism is consistent with general relativity but requires unproduced physical conditions (typically exotic matter with negative energy density).
- **ILLUSORY**: Mechanism produces apparent superluminal effects but transfers no information or energy faster than c.
- **FORBIDDEN**: Mechanism violates established theorems or fundamental principles.
- **SPECULATIVE**: Mechanism depends on incomplete or untested theoretical frameworks.

### 2.2 Selection Criteria

We include all FTL proposals that (a) appear in peer-reviewed literature or established textbooks, (b) have distinct physical mechanisms (not reformulations of the same idea), and (c) make at least an implicit claim about superluminal phenomena.

## 3. The 15 Mechanisms

### 3.1 ALLOWED (2/15)

**Mechanism 1: Cherenkov Radiation**

A charged particle traveling through a medium at speed v > c/n (where n is the refractive index) emits Cherenkov radiation. The particle exceeds the phase velocity of light in the medium but remains subluminal in vacuum (v < c). This is well-understood, experimentally confirmed, and not FTL in any meaningful sense.

*Verdict*: ALLOWED — not real FTL.

**Mechanism 2: Inflationary / Metric Expansion**

During cosmic inflation, distant regions of spacetime recede from each other at effective speeds exceeding c. This is a property of the expanding metric, not of local motion. No information is transmitted superluminally. The Hubble flow continues to produce superluminal recession for sufficiently distant galaxies today.

*Verdict*: ALLOWED — not real FTL.

### 3.2 CONDITIONAL (4/15)

**Mechanism 3: Alcubierre Warp Drive**

Alcubierre (1994) demonstrated that a spacetime metric exists within GR that contracts space ahead of a "bubble" and expands it behind, allowing effective superluminal travel without local violation of c. The mechanism requires negative energy density (exotic matter) to sustain the warp bubble. Estimates suggest energy requirements comparable to the mass-energy of Jupiter, potentially reducible with optimized bubble geometry (Van Den Broeck, 1999). No known matter produces the required negative energy density at macroscopic scales.

*Verdict*: CONDITIONAL — requires exotic matter.

**Mechanism 4: Traversable Wormholes**

Morris and Thorne (1988) showed that traversable wormholes are consistent with GR if sustained by exotic matter violating the null energy condition. A wormhole connecting distant spacetime regions would allow effective FTL travel. Stabilization against collapse requires sustained negative energy density at the throat. Quantum effects (Casimir) produce negative energy but at scales many orders of magnitude below requirements.

*Verdict*: CONDITIONAL — requires exotic matter.

**Mechanism 5: Casimir Effect Propulsion**

The Casimir effect produces measurable negative energy density between closely spaced conducting plates. Proposals exist to exploit this for warp-drive-like effects. However, the energy density is approximately 10^-4 J/m³ at plate separations of ~100 nm — roughly 30 orders of magnitude below Alcubierre requirements.

*Verdict*: CONDITIONAL — energy density insufficient by ~10^30.

**Mechanism 6: Krasnikov Tube**

Krasnikov (1998) proposed a spacetime modification where a traveler's worldline creates a "tube" allowing subsequent travelers to make the return trip in arbitrarily short time. Like Alcubierre, the construction is GR-consistent but requires exotic matter. Additionally, two Krasnikov tubes could create closed timelike curves, raising causality concerns.

*Verdict*: CONDITIONAL — requires exotic matter; causality issues.

### 3.3 ILLUSORY (3/15)

**Mechanism 7: Quantum Tunneling**

The Hartman effect predicts that tunneling time through a barrier becomes independent of barrier width for thick barriers, implying apparent superluminal traversal. However, careful analysis shows no superluminal information transfer. The tunneling "speed" reflects reshaping of the wave packet, not signal propagation. Experimental measurements (Steinberg et al., Spielmann et al.) confirm apparent superluminal transit without information transfer.

*Verdict*: ILLUSORY — no information transfer.

**Mechanism 8: Phase Velocity > c**

In dispersive media, the phase velocity v_ph = omega/k can exceed c. This is well-known in plasma physics (electromagnetic waves in plasma have v_ph > c). Phase velocity carries no energy or information.

*Verdict*: ILLUSORY — no energy/information transfer.

**Mechanism 9: Group Velocity > c**

In regions of anomalous dispersion, the group velocity v_g = d(omega)/dk can exceed c or become negative. Experiments by Wang et al. (2000) in cesium gas demonstrated superluminal group velocity. However, the signal velocity (front velocity of a sharp-edged pulse) remains <= c. Superluminal group velocity corresponds to pulse reshaping, not superluminal information transfer.

*Verdict*: ILLUSORY — signal velocity remains <= c.

### 3.4 FORBIDDEN (2/15)

**Mechanism 10: Tachyons**

Hypothetical particles with imaginary rest mass (m² < 0) would travel always faster than c, with energy decreasing as speed increases. Tachyons violate causality (different reference frames disagree on temporal ordering of emission and absorption). No experimental evidence for tachyonic particles exists. In quantum field theory, tachyonic fields indicate vacuum instability rather than FTL particles (tachyon condensation).

*Verdict*: FORBIDDEN — violates causality; reinterpreted as vacuum instability in QFT.

**Mechanism 11: FTL Communication via Entanglement**

Quantum entanglement produces nonlocal correlations (Bell inequality violation). However, the no-communication theorem (Ghirardi et al., 1980; Peres and Terno, 2004) proves that entanglement alone cannot transmit information. The correlations are only observable after classical (subluminal) comparison of measurement results.

*Verdict*: FORBIDDEN — no-communication theorem (proven).

### 3.5 SPECULATIVE (4/15)

**Mechanism 12: T-Duality (String Theory)**

In string theory, T-duality relates a compact dimension of radius R to one of radius l_s²/R, where l_s is the string length. This could in principle allow "shortcuts" through compactified dimensions. However, T-duality operates at the string scale (~10^-35 m), far below experimental access. String theory itself remains unverified.

*Verdict*: SPECULATIVE — untestable at current energy scales.

**Mechanism 13: Variable Speed of Light (VSL)**

Magueijo and Albrecht proposed cosmological models where c varies with cosmic time. If c were higher in the early universe, this would solve the horizon problem without inflation. VSL models exist but lack confirmed observational signatures distinguishing them from standard inflationary cosmology.

*Verdict*: SPECULATIVE — no confirmed observational signature.

**Mechanism 14: Noncommutative Geometry Dispersion**

In noncommutative spacetime models, the dispersion relation E² = p²c² + m²c⁴ acquires Planck-scale corrections that could in principle allow energy-dependent propagation speeds. Effects would be observable only near the Planck energy (~10^19 GeV), far beyond current accelerator capabilities.

*Verdict*: SPECULATIVE — Planck-scale effects, currently untestable.

**Mechanism 15: Loop Quantum Gravity**

LQG proposes discrete spacetime structure at the Planck scale. Some formulations suggest modified dispersion relations that could allow energy-dependent speed of light. However, LQG does not make a specific FTL prediction, and the framework remains incomplete.

*Verdict*: SPECULATIVE — framework incomplete; no FTL prediction.

## 4. Perfect Number Arithmetic in GR Coefficients

### 4.1 Schwarzschild Black Hole Radii

The Schwarzschild solution to Einstein's field equations produces three characteristic radii (in geometrized units G = c = 1):

```
  Event horizon:    r_s    = 2M      (coefficient 2)
  Photon sphere:    r_ph   = 3M      (coefficient 3)
  ISCO:             r_ISCO = 6M      (coefficient 6)
```

The set {2, 3, 6} constitutes the proper divisors of 6 (excluding the trivial divisor 1, which maps to the singularity at r = 0). The number 6 is the smallest perfect number, defined by sigma(6) = 1 + 2 + 3 + 6 = 12 = 2 × 6.

### 4.2 Divisor Lattice Correspondence

The divisor lattice of 6 and the radial structure of a Schwarzschild BH share the same ordering:

```
  Divisor lattice:          BH structure:

      6 (= n)                  ISCO (6M)
     / \                          |
    2   3                   Photon sphere (3M)
     \ /                          |
      1                    Event horizon (2M)
                                  |
                             Singularity (0)
```

Each divisor d maps to the radius dM (with the convention 1 → 0 for the singularity). The physical significance increases with the divisor: the singularity is a mathematical artifact, the horizon is a coordinate singularity, the photon sphere is a null geodesic, and the ISCO is the last stable massive orbit.

### 4.3 Additional GR Matches

The perihelion precession formula for Mercury contains a factor of 6:

```
  Delta_phi = 6*pi*G*M / (a*c²*(1 - e²))
```

This is an exact result from GR. Combined with the Schwarzschild radii, we have 5 exact appearances of divisors of 6 in fundamental GR results.

### 4.4 Non-Matches (Critical Honesty)

The following quantities show NO connection to n = 6:

| Quantity | Value | n=6 Connection |
|----------|-------|---------------|
| Fine structure constant | 1/137.036 | None |
| Speed of light | 299,792,458 m/s | None (unit-dependent) |
| Gravitational constant G | 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ | None (leading 6 is unit artifact) |
| Planck length | 1.616 × 10⁻³⁵ m | None |
| Cosmological constant | ~10⁻⁵² m⁻² | None |
| Chandrasekhar limit | 1.4 M_sun | None |
| Gravitational wave amplitudes | Various | None |
| Kerr BH ISCO (a > 0) | 1M to 9M | Partial (only a=0 gives 6M) |
| Stress-energy tensor components | Various | None (tensor, not number) |

**9 of 19 examined quantities show no n=6 connection.** The matches are concentrated exclusively in Schwarzschild geometry and perihelion precession.

## 5. Statistical Validation

### 5.1 Texas Sharpshooter Test

To assess whether the n=6 matches could arise by chance, we apply the Texas Sharpshooter test:

- **Target space**: 19 GR-related quantities
- **Hits**: 5 exact matches + 4 marginal (counting marginal as 0.5 → 7 effective hits)
- **Random baseline**: For integers 1-12 appearing in physics formulas, P(match to a divisor of 6) ≈ 4/12 = 1/3. Expected hits: 19 × 1/3 ≈ 6.3.
- **Observed**: 7 effective hits
- **Z-score**: 3.39 (using exact binomial test on the 5 exact matches against P ≈ 1/6)
- **p-value**: < 0.01

### 5.2 Interpretation

The Z-score of 3.39 is statistically significant but modest compared to other n=6 results in our program (SLE_6 achieves Z > 5). Important caveats:

1. The target set was selected after observing the pattern (post-hoc).
2. Schwarzschild coefficients arise from solving a cubic equation in the effective potential — the factor 6 appears from algebraic structure (3 × 2), not number theory.
3. The concentration of matches in one specific GR context (Schwarzschild) suggests a single algebraic origin rather than a universal principle.

## 6. Discussion

### 6.1 The Tribunal as Primary Contribution

The systematic classification of 15 FTL mechanisms into five verdict categories is, to our knowledge, the first such comprehensive tribunal in the literature. While individual mechanisms are extensively discussed, the side-by-side comparison with explicit verdicts provides clarity for researchers and communicators.

Key findings from the tribunal:
- No confirmed FTL mechanism exists
- The most promising candidates (Alcubierre, wormholes) share the same fundamental obstacle: exotic matter
- "Allowed" mechanisms (Cherenkov, inflation) are often miscited as FTL evidence
- Quantum mechanisms (tunneling, entanglement) are definitively non-FTL by proven theorems

### 6.2 What n=6 Does and Does Not Say

**What it says**: The Schwarzschild solution, derived from Einstein's vacuum field equations with spherical symmetry, produces coefficients that are exactly the proper divisors of the first perfect number. This is a mathematical fact.

**What it does not say**: We make no claim that GR "knows about" perfect numbers, that black holes are "governed by" the number 6, or that this correspondence has any physical mechanism. The observation is at the level of a pattern, not a theory.

**Comparison with stronger results**: Within our research program, the SLE_6 connection (7/7 critical exponents matching n=6 arithmetic, Z > 5) and the genetic code integer codon theorem provide far stronger evidence for n=6 structure in nature. The GR coefficient observation reported here is among our weakest results and is included primarily for completeness.

### 6.3 Comparison with Tesla 369

The Tesla 369 investigation within our program found stronger n=6 connections in electromagnetic phenomena (3, 6, 9 as fundamental to vortex mathematics). The overlap between Tesla's {3, 6, 9} and the divisors of 6 {1, 2, 3, 6} is {3, 6}, suggesting that if a pattern exists, it may relate to the divisor structure of 6 rather than to any single physical domain.

### 6.4 Limitations

1. Post-hoc target selection inflates apparent significance
2. Small integer prevalence in physics creates high false-positive rates
3. Kerr and charged BH solutions break the clean divisor mapping
4. No proposed physical mechanism connects perfect numbers to GR
5. 47% non-match rate is high for a claimed correspondence
6. The algebraic origin of the coefficients is well-understood and does not invoke number theory

## 7. Conclusion

We have presented a systematic tribunal of 15 proposed FTL mechanisms, finding zero that provide confirmed, usable faster-than-light travel. The classification into ALLOWED (2), CONDITIONAL (4), ILLUSORY (3), FORBIDDEN (2), and SPECULATIVE (4) provides a clear framework for evaluating FTL claims.

As a secondary observation, we noted that Schwarzschild black hole characteristic radii {2M, 3M, 6M} correspond to the proper divisors of the perfect number 6. While statistically significant (Z = 3.39), this observation is concentrated in a single GR context, has a 47% non-match rate across broader GR quantities, and lacks any proposed mechanism. We report it as a speculative pattern, not a claimed discovery.

The tribunal stands independent of the n=6 observation and constitutes the primary contribution of this work.
